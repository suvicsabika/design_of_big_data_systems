## Functional Requirements (FR)

| ID  | Requirement        | Architectural Significance |
|-----|--------------------|----------------------------|
| FR1 | Event Creation      | Requires content management, publishing workflows, and role-based access control for organizers. |
| FR2 | Ticket Sales        | Core business capability; drives transactional processing, payment integration, and digital ticket generation. |
| FR3 | Admission Control   | Requires real-time or near-real-time ticket validation, mobile scanning support, and secure validation mechanisms. |
| FR4 | Dynamic Pricing     | Requires pricing rules engine, real-time data processing, and flexible business logic services. |
| FR5 | Seat Management     | Has strong impact on data consistency, concurrency control, and inventory reservation mechanisms. |
| FR6 | Notifications       | Requires integration with external communication providers and support for asynchronous event-driven workflows. |
| FR7 | Analytics           | Requires collection and processing of operational and business data, often through separate analytical pipelines. |
| FR8 | Marketing Campaigns | Implies customer segmentation, recommendation capabilities, and integration with campaign delivery components. |

## Non-Functional Requirements (NFR)

| ID   | Quality Attribute   | Architectural Significance |
|------|----------------------|----------------------------|
| NFR1 | Performance          | Requires low-latency services, optimized APIs, caching, and efficient purchase workflows. |
| NFR2 | Scalability          | Requires horizontally scalable components, load balancing, distributed services, and elastic infrastructure. |
| NFR3 | Availability         | Requires fault tolerance, redundancy, multi-region or globally distributed deployment, and resilient recovery mechanisms. |
| NFR4 | Security             | Requires encryption, secure payment handling, strong authentication, authorization, and secure secret management. |
| NFR5 | Maintainability      | Encourages modular design, clear service boundaries, CI/CD support, and observability. |
| NFR6 | Integration          | Requires stable APIs, adapters, and loose coupling with third-party providers. |
| NFR7 | Consistency          | Critically affects storage and transaction design to prevent overselling and ensure inventory correctness. |
| NFR8 | Audit & Compliance   | Requires logging, traceability, retention policies, and privacy-aware data handling. |
